B U I L D

Pronounciation:
[bild]
Definition 1 - Verb (used with object):
to construct by assembling and joining parts or materials
Example: He built a green house.
to establish, increase or strengthen
Example: She built up his hopes of a new car.
to mold, form, or create
Example: This school will build boys into men.
to base, found
Example: A relationship built on trust.
Definition 2 - Verb (used without object):
to engage in the art, practice or business of building
Example: He is a builder of home tools.
to form or construct a plan, system of thought
Example: He built on the philosophies of the past.
to increase or develop toward a maximum, as of intensity, tempo, or magnitude
Example: The drama builds steadily toward a climax.
Definition 3 - Noun:
the physical structure, especially of a person; physique; figure
Example: He had a strong build.
the manner or form of construction
Example: The house was of modern build
computer versions of programs, process of producing a software, new version or update
Example: It was a frequent, incremental build of data
masonry, a vertical joint or the vertical dimension of a stone laid on its bed
Example: The build of the mason

Origins:

Before 1150, Middle English bilden, derivative of bold

Origin of Root Words:

Proto-Germanic

Synonoms:

assemble, erect, evolve, frame, physique, shape, model, begin

Antonymns:

destroy,conclude, neglect, ruin, decelerate, overthrow, demolish
